Cold Gas Thrusters
Back to Navigation

Electric thrusters work by accelerating propellant atoms to higher velocities than can be obtained by chemical propulsion. In chemical propulsion, the energy produced is limited by how much energy there is in the chemical bonds of the propellant. The combustion products are heated by the energy released in the breaking of each chemical bond while burning, and a rocket nozzle converts much of that thermal energy into directed particle velocity to produce thrust. The higher the velocity, the higher the specific impulse and thrust from a given amount of propellant.
